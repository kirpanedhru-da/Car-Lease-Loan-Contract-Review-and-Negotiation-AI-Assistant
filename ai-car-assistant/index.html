Frequently Asked Questions

What is a "Money Factor" and how do I calculate the interest rate?

The Money Factor (MF) determines the financing charge on a lease. To convert it to a standard Annual Percentage Rate (APR), simply multiply the Money Factor by 2,400. For example, a Money Factor of 0.0025 equals an APR of 6.0%. Always ask the dealer for the "buy rate".

What are the most common hidden fees to watch out for?

Dealerships often add unnecessary "accessories" or "protection packages" (like VIN etching, nitrogen tires, or fabric protection) that can inflate your capitalized cost. Additionally, watch out for inflated acquisition fees and excessive disposition fees upon returning the vehicle.

Is it better to lease or buy?

It depends on your lifestyle. Leasing is great if you want lower monthly payments, always want a vehicle under warranty, and drive an average amount (10k-12k miles/year). Buying is generally better for long-term wealth building and if you drive extensively or plan to keep the car for more than 5 years.

Can I negotiate the Residual Value?

No, the Residual Value is strictly set by the bank or leasing company and cannot be changed by the dealer. However, you CAN heavily negotiate the "Capitalized Cost" (the actual selling price of the car) and the Money Factor markup!
